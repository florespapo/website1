Overview

Monkeypox Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, which belongs to the Orthopoxvirus genus â€” the same family as the smallpox virus (variola). Although related to smallpox, monkeypox is usually less severe. First identified in 1958 in monkeys (hence the name). The first human case was recorded in 1970 in the Democratic Republic of Congo. It occurs primarily in Central and West Africa but has spread to non-endemic countries since 2022. The Philippines has been actively monitoring and responding to the cases reported in the country to control its spread and protect public health.

Symptoms

The following symptoms are:

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle Aches
  • Back Pain
  • Fatigue
  • Swollen lymph nodes (distinguishing feature from smallpox)
  • Rash (starts as flat lesions > raised > vesicles > pustules > scabs)
  • Rash often starts on the face, then spreads to other parts, including palms, soles, and mucous membranes

Transmission

  • Animal-to-human transmission: contact with infected animals (bites, scratches, bush meat, fluids)
  • Human-to-human transmission: Direct contact with skin lesions, body fluids, Respiratory droplets (prolonged face-to-face contact), Contaminated materials (bedding, clothing), Intimate or sexual contact in some outbreaks.

The Department of Health (DOH) continues to monitor active cases. Most patients have shown mild to moderate symptoms and have recovered fully. No fatalities have been reported so far.

Prevention Measures

  • Regular hand washing with soap and water
  • Avoiding contact with infected individuals
  • Using protective equipment in healthcare settings
  • Immediate medical attention if symptoms appear
  • Following official DOH guidelines
